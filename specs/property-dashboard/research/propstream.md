# PropStream (propstream.com) — Competitive Teardown for an Investment-Property Analytics App

*Method note: propstream.com and most review sites block automated fetchers, so this analysis is assembled from ~12 web-search passes over PropStream's public marketing pages, help-video library pages, press releases (BusinessWire/RISMedia/StockTitan), and third-party reviews (SparkRental, REsimpli, RealEstateSkills, KDS Development, Trustpilot/G2/Capterra summaries). Specific claims are sourced at the end; anything inferred is flagged.*

---

## 1. Information architecture

**Marketing site.** Organized around three axes:
- **Product**: Features (`/propstream-features`), Pricing (`/pricing`), dedicated feature landing pages that each own a URL and an SEO keyword — `/comps-for-real-estate`, `/lead-automator`, `/predictive-ai`, `/propstream-intelligence`, `/real-estate-ai`, `/campaigns`, `/propstream-connect`, `/propstream-mobile-app`.
- **Audience ("Industry Focus")**: persona landing pages — `/real-estate-investors`, `/real-estate-agent(s)`, `/real-estate-brokers`, plus persona-split blogs (`/real-estate-investor-blog/...` vs `/real-estate-agent-blog/...`).
- **Education/support**: PropStream Academy (`/propstream-academy`, separate `academy.propstream.com` LMS on Docebo), a Help Video Library where *every tutorial video is its own indexed page* (`/how-to-run-comps-in-propstream`, `/how-to-search-with-filters`, `/how-to-utilize-analysis-tool`, `/how-to-set-up-alerts-and-automations`), a news/PR hub (`/news/...`), and FAQ.

**In-product organization.** The app is map-first: a persistent search bar (address / APN / ZIP / city / county) with a **Filter panel (165+ filters)** to its right, a Draw tool on the map, and "Quick Lists" (pre-built distress segments like pre-foreclosures, zombie properties, failed listings) as one-click entry points. Saved Searches pin above the map. Saved properties collect into lists ("My Properties"), which feed downstream modules: skip tracing → marketing campaigns → Lead Automator monitoring. The data model is essentially **Search → List → Property → Action**, and the whole product is arranged along that pipeline.

## 2. Core user flows

**Find a property.** Three entry paths: (a) type an address/APN/ZIP/county; (b) pick a Quick List (pre-foreclosure, liens, absentee, etc.) that auto-populates for the targeted area; (c) Draw tool — Free Draw / Circle / Rectangle, up to 5,000 sq mi. Filters live in a slide-out panel with a **"Find a Filter" keyword search**, a live count of matching properties at the top of the panel (so users tune breadth in real time), a "Show All Applied Filters" view, and an **On-Market/Off-Market toggle** directly in the search bar. The 2025 map redesign shows up to **250 pins at once, each pin displaying a dollar value** (AVM estimate or list price), defaults to map view rather than satellite, and pops "Map Cards" — a glanceable overview with equity, comps, estimated rent, and property classification — on pin click. Bookmarking happens inline from search/map cards without opening the detail page.

**Property detail page.** A single scrolling record with tabbed/sectioned data: property characteristics; owner details (name, mailing address, corporate/trust resolution, absentee status); mortgage and transaction history; tax/assessment; pre-foreclosure/auction/lien/bankruptcy flags; estimated value and estimated equity; estimated rent. A **"Comparables & Nearby Listings" tab** lives on the detail page, as does the launcher for the Rehab Calculator (property must be saved first) and, since 2025, an embedded **PropStream Intelligence Chat** — an AI assistant scoped to that property that answers questions like "What's the total portfolio value for this owner?"

**Run comps.** Comps auto-run with defaults (sold within 12 months, within 0.5 mi, ±20% sq ft) and are then user-adjustable (distance, sale date, sq ft, etc.). Users toggle the data source — **public-record comps rendered green, MLS comps blue, or blended** — check/uncheck individual comps, and get a live summary strip (count selected, average sale price, average $/sq ft). "View Report" generates a client-ready PDF of the selected comps.

## 3. Content depth

**Coverage claims:** 160M+ U.S. properties, multi-sourced from county recordings, MLS, and private sources; component figures cited publicly include ~70M MLS records, 308M deed/sales records, 185M mortgage records, and 150M involuntary liens. Owned by Stewart Information Services (title-data giant), which underwrites the data story.

**Property-record depth:** ownership (individual/LLC/corp/trust, absentee, years owned), mailing addresses, mortgage balances and open-lien detail, involuntary liens, pre-foreclosure/auction status and dates, bankruptcy, divorce, tax delinquency, transaction/deed history, tax assessments, MLS status (active/pending/expired/failed listings), estimated value (AI AVM), **estimated equity and equity %**, estimated rent, and a predictive **"Foreclosure Factor"** propensity score (Very Low → Very High likelihood of default within 12 months).

**Financial metrics supported:** Rental ROI Calculator — inputs for purchase price, improvements, units, down payment %, rate, term, rent, vacancy, taxes, insurance, management, maintenance, utilities, HOA; outputs **cash flow, mortgage payment, NOI, cap rate, cash-on-cash ROI, GRM, appreciation/equity growth, financing sensitivity comparisons, and multi-year (30+ yr) pro-forma projections**. Fix & Flip Analyzer outputs profit potential and **maximum allowable offer (MAO)**. ADU calculator for addition costs. Notably **absent from public materials: DSCR, IRR, NPV, WACC** — their analytics stop at investor-folk metrics; institutional underwriting metrics are not part of the story. That is exactly the white space a CAP/NOI/DSCR/IRR/NPV/WACC dashboard occupies.

**Rehab estimates:** three-tier input UX (Complete Remodel by Area for beginners → Popular Projects → Individual Line Items for pros), materials/quality auto-selected from property location and characteristics, and **geo-specific labor + material costs producing two parallel estimates: DIY cost vs. contractor cost**.

## 4. Onboarding, CTAs, conversion

- **7-day free trial including 50 free leads** (saves/exports) — the trial is the primary CTA everywhere; there is no permanent free tier. Card required; affiliates promote extended trials (e.g., "30 days free" via partner links), showing trial length is a lever they trade for distribution.
- Paid tiers (2025–26 restructure): **Essentials $99/mo, Pro $199/mo, Elite $699/mo**, annual discount. Classic land-and-expand: skip tracing 12¢/hit and Lead Automator $27/mo as Essentials add-ons but bundled free into Pro/Elite; team seats $30/mo; postcards from $0.48, emails $0.02 — a usage-revenue layer atop subscription.
- **First-run experience is human + content heavy**: a dedicated `/7-day-free-trial-getting-started` walkthrough video, two live onboarding sessions per day **capped at 20 attendees**, daily webinars, and the free Academy (separate account) with on-demand courses and downloadable Certificates of Completion — certification doubles as social/loyalty marketing.
- Conversion surface area is enormous: every blog post, help video page, and PR item ends in the trial CTA; persona landing pages pitch the same product with role-specific framing.

## 5. Category-specific tooling

- **Comps:** dual-source (public record + MLS) with color-coded provenance, adjustable criteria, select/deselect per comp, live averages, PDF report export. Mobile comps supported.
- **Rehab calculator:** described above — its differentiators are geo-priced line items and the DIY-vs-contractor dual estimate.
- **List building:** 165+ filters across characteristics, MLS status, owner, financial (value/equity %/mortgage balance), and distress; Quick Lists; list stacking (properties appearing on multiple distress lists); import your own lists.
- **Alerts/monitoring:** **Lead Automator** monitors up to 25,000 properties daily, auto-adds new matches, auto-removes dead leads, and emails alerts the moment a lead's status changes — "self-cleaning lists" as a paid add-on.
- **Reports:** comp PDF reports, customizable property reports (ownership, liens, mortgage), campaign tracking dashboards; CSV export of lists (metered by plan).
- **Outreach (adjacent category):** in-platform skip tracing with DNC/litigator flags, postcards, email drip campaigns, landing pages, Click-to-Dial and Dialer Campaigns (post BatchLeads/BatchDialer acquisition, 2026) — bundled as "PropStream Connect."
- **AI layer:** PropStream Intelligence — heat maps (estimated values, MLS stats, price growth, rental values), Foreclosure Factor propensity score, pre-MLS "likely seller" prediction, and the per-property AI chat assistant (multilingual).

## 6. Trust patterns

- **Quantified data provenance** is the core trust move: "160M+ properties," itemized record counts (deeds, mortgages, liens, MLS), "multi-sourced from county recordings, MLS, and private sources," and the Stewart Title parentage — the pitch is data pedigree, not testimonials alone.
- **Named methodology-ish framing** without true methodology: AVM is "AI-generated," Foreclosure Factor described as "data sources and AI algorithms" with a plain-English score band (Very Low→Very High). Transparent enough to feel rigorous, vague enough to protect the model — no published error rates or accuracy studies.
- **Third-party ratings:** Trustpilot ~4.2/5 ("Great," ~270+ reviews), G2 4.7/5 (~193 reviews), Capterra/SoftwareAdvice listings; press placements (RISMedia cover feature for the CEO, BusinessWire releases picked up by Yahoo Finance/Morningstar) manufacture authority.
- **Compliance signaling as trust:** DNC/litigator flags on skip-traced contacts, CAN-SPAM-compliant email — turning legal risk-reduction into a feature.
- **Education as credibility:** free Academy with certificates positions them as the category teacher, not just a vendor.

## 7. Experiential layer

- **Design language:** dark navy/near-black grounds with a warm orange/amber accent for CTAs and the logo mark, white content cards, and screenshot-forward sections (product UI framed in device/browser chrome). In-app, the aesthetic is utilitarian and **data-dense** — filter panels, table-like comp lists, stat strips — a workbench, not a lifestyle product. Reviewers consistently describe a "decent" but busy UI with a real learning curve. *(Palette specifics partly inferred from brand assets; density/learning-curve from reviews.)*
- **Hero/first impression:** value-proposition-led ("nationwide data + tools to find, analyze, and market to off-market deals"), coverage number up top, trial CTA immediately, and a product screenshot of the map interface as hero art. Persona pages repeat the pattern with role-specific headlines.
- **Property-page hierarchy:** photo + headline stats (beds/baths/sq ft/year) up top, then stacked disclosure sections (owner → mortgage/liens → tax → transactions → MLS), with analysis actions (Comps tab, Rehab Calculator, AI chat) attached to the record rather than in a separate module — analysis lives *on* the property.
- **Map as the visual centerpiece:** the 2025 "New Visual Experience" release is explicitly a design story — 250 value-labeled pins, cleaner default map style (map view over satellite), richer hover/click "Map Cards," inline bookmarking, redesigned Draw and Analytics icons. They market UI polish via press release, which tells you the interface had accumulated debt and that customers noticed.
- **Motion/interaction:** live-updating result counts as filters change, auto-refreshing results when a drawn boundary is adjusted, real-time bookmark feedback, color-coded comp provenance (green/blue), and heat-map overlays — interaction patterns oriented to *tightening a funnel of thousands of parcels down to a call list*.

## Steal-worthy patterns

- **Auto-run analysis with sane defaults, then expose the dials.** Comps compute instantly on defaults (12 mo / 0.5 mi / ±20% sq ft) with every parameter adjustable after — apply the same to CAP/DSCR/IRR: show a defensible default underwrite immediately, let users tune assumptions second.
- **Color-coded data provenance.** Green = public record, blue = MLS. In an underwriting dashboard, visually tag every input as "county record / MLS / user-entered / model-estimated" — it builds trust and speeds review.
- **Live result/impact counts while filtering.** The filter panel's running match count (and a "find a filter" search over 165+ filters) keeps users oriented in a dense control surface.
- **Value-labeled map pins + glanceable "map cards."** Pins showing a dollar figure (AVM or price) and click-cards with equity/rent/comps summary make the map itself an analysis surface, not just navigation.
- **Dual-scenario estimates.** DIY vs. contractor rehab costs presented side-by-side is a great template: financed vs. all-cash, or three financing scenarios, rendered as parallel columns rather than requiring re-entry.
- **Tiered input complexity (beginner/intermediate/pro).** Rehab Estimator's "remodel by area → popular projects → individual line items" ladder is a clean solution to serving novices and pros in one tool; mirror it for underwriting assumptions.
- **Self-cleaning monitored lists with change alerts.** Lead Automator's "we add new matches and remove dead ones daily, and ping you on status change" pattern maps directly to a watched-portfolio/comparison view whose metrics re-run when data changes.
- **Every tutorial as an indexed public page + itemized data-coverage numbers.** The help-video-per-URL library and the "308M deeds / 185M mortgages" style enumeration are cheap, high-yield SEO and trust plays.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- **No institutional-grade underwriting metrics.** Public materials stop at cap rate, cash-on-cash, GRM, NOI, and MAO — no DSCR, IRR, NPV, or WACC, and no side-by-side **multi-property comparison view**; analysis is one property at a time. This is the clearest opening for a comparison-first underwriting dashboard.
- **Complexity tax.** 165+ filters, add-on stacking, and a lead-gen-centric IA create a documented learning curve (daily onboarding webinars exist because the product needs them); analysis tools are buried behind "save the property first" steps and separate windows.
- **Data reliability varies by county.** Recurring user reports of stale or missing records in specific regions, with no published accuracy metrics or per-market coverage disclosure — coverage is claimed in aggregate only.
- **Pricing creep and paywalled essentials.** $99 base quickly becomes $150–250+ once Lead Automator, skip tracing, seats, and marketing spend are added; no free tier and metered exports frustrate small users (visible in Trustpilot 4.2 vs. G2 4.7 split).
- **Analytics are a means to outreach, not the product.** The whole pipeline optimizes for "build a call list"; investors who want rigorous hold/return modeling, sensitivity analysis, or portfolio-level views must export to spreadsheets — the workflow ends exactly where an analytics-first product should begin.
