Methodology
Definitions & decision rules

WACC — the hurdle rate

Equity% × opportunity cost of equity + Debt% × cost of debt × (1 − tax rate). Here: 30% × 15% + 70% × 6.5% × 0.72 = 7.78%.

IRR — internal rate of return

The discount rate at which the NPV of the five-year series equals zero — the deal's expected annualized return. Given a constant CAP rate, IRR holds constant for any combination of NOI and purchase price.

NPV — net present value

NPV = Σ CFt / (1 + r)t − initial investment, discounted at WACC. A positive NPV means the deal's return exceeds the cost of capital — IRR will be greater than WACC, and the project clears the bar.

NPV = 0 — the break-even cost of capital

When the NPV of all cash flows equals 0, the project's expected cash flows are just enough to cover the cash flows created by the cost of capital (WACC). Setting NPV to 0 lets you evaluate the true rate of return (IRR) against WACC: if IRR exceeds WACC, the project clears the bar.

Why (1 + r)^t

(1 + r)^t accounts for the compounding effect of the discount rate over time — the factor that converts future values to present values. CFt is the cash flow in year t, r is the discount rate, t is the year (1 to hold).
